Derek and I met in April 2013 and our story is pretty simple. We happened to meet on a night when we both should have been studying for finals (Derek especially). My friends invited me along on a college night with their church to visit Airwalk, a trampoline arena in Birmingham, followed by eating at The Cheesecake Factory. On the way home from Birmingham, Derek and I talked about our favorite music until we were the only ones still awake. A week after the college trip, we met at Sokol Park where he taught me how to slackline, and we learned that I was allergic to poison ivy. Later in our relationship, I asked Derek when he was first interested in me and he replied, "You were the only one who wouldn't jump in the foam pit!" Gosh, he's romantic.

For the next couple of years after we started dating, Derek and I both struggled through college together. We would spend our date nights studying late, living off of vending machine food and pizza. We each had our hit-rock-bottom-where-there-is-no-sunshine-and-puppies-left-in-the-world moments, but we made it with each other's support and a lot of prayer. By going through college with Derek, I learned that he could handle me when I'm stressed, haven't slept, haven't eaten, and gone without makeup. That's love. Between semesters we loved to travel to get away for a while. Atlanta, Stone Mountain, Gatlinburg, North Carolina, Colorado, Disney World, and Washington D. C. were some of the places we were able to visit in our down time. While I still had another year in college, Derek graduated and got a job working as a software developer. However, that didn't stop me from abusing his Microsoft Office skills. A month before I graduated college, Derek told me he had dinner reservations for the upcoming Friday night. He took me to a barn back at Sokol Park (no poison ivy this time) where he had built a timeline of our relationship using pictures of us from the past three and a half years. He put so much work into the setup, because that's just Derek. We are very different from each other. Where he is loud, I am quiet. He likes computers, and I like books. He is neat, and I am messy. I like scary shows and he is a big baby. He spends a lot of time making sure things are perfect where I give up after two minutes. Among all our differences, we work to complement each other where we need it the most. We are excited about this journey and hope you can join us as we celebrate this new chapter in our lives!
